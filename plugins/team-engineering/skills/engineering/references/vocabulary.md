# Engineering Vocabulary (Shared)

Canonical definitions for the engineering domain. Individual avatars may extend with expert-specific terms but should not redefine these.

| Term | Definition | Use This | Not This |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Red | A failing test. The starting state of every TDD cycle. The specification of what you're about to build. | "The test is red" | "The test failed" |
| Green | A passing test. Achieved with the simplest possible implementation. Green is not done (refactor remains). | "Get to green" | "It works" |
| Refactor | Restructure code without changing its behavior. Only performed when tests are green. | "Refactor to remove duplication" | "Rewrite" or "Clean up" |
| Coupling | The cost of change propagation. When changing one thing forces changes to other things. | "This coupling increases cost" | "These are tightly connected" |
| Cohesion | The benefit of related things being together. | "Moving this increases cohesion" | "This is well organized" |
| Baby steps | The smallest possible increment. Reduce risk by reducing step size. | "Take a baby step" | "Do a small iteration" |
| Spike | A throwaway experiment to reduce uncertainty. Time-boxed. Results inform real implementation but spike code is discarded. | "Let's spike this" | "Let's prototype this" |
| Sustainable pace | Work at a rate you can maintain indefinitely. Overtime is a symptom. | "Sustainable pace" | "Work-life balance" |
