# Gene Kim Vocabulary

| Term | Definition | Use This | Not This |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Three Ways | Flow (optimize whole system), Feedback (shorten loops), Continuous Learning (experiment). The DevOps foundation. | "The Three Ways" | "DevOps practices" |
| Four Types of Work | Business projects, internal projects, changes, unplanned work. Track all four. | "Four Types of Work" | "Task categories" |
| Five Ideals | Locality/Simplicity, Focus/Flow/Joy, Improve Daily Work, Psychological Safety, Customer Focus. | "The Five Ideals" | "DevOps principles" |
| Unplanned work | Firefighting. The most destructive type of work. Steals capacity and creates more of itself. | "Unplanned work" | "Interruptions" or "bugs" |
| WIP | Work in progress. Too much = long lead times. Limit it ruthlessly. | "WIP" or "work in progress" | "Backlog" |
| Bottleneck | The single constraint limiting system throughput. From Goldratt. | "The bottleneck" | "Slow team" or "blocker" |
| Value stream | The entire flow from business request to customer value delivered. Includes all wait time. | "Value stream" | "Process" or "workflow" |
| Lead time | Time from work requested to work delivered to customer. The primary flow metric. | "Lead time" | "Cycle time" (different metric) |
| Deployment pipeline | Fully automated path from code commit to production deployment. No manual steps. | "Deployment pipeline" | "CI/CD" (too vague) |
| Telemetry | Automated, continuous measurement of application, infrastructure, and business metrics. | "Telemetry" | "Monitoring" (too passive) |
| Blameless postmortem | Investigation of failure focused on system causes, not individual blame. Everyone contributes safely. | "Blameless postmortem" | "Retrospective" (different scope) |
| Psychological safety | Team members feel safe to take risks, fail, experiment, and speak up without fear. | "Psychological safety" | "Nice culture" or "trust" |
| Locality and simplicity | Systems decomposed so changes are local and teams work independently. | "Locality and simplicity" | "Microservices" (too specific) |
| Chaos engineering | Deliberate injection of failure to build resilience and expose weaknesses before they cause outages. | "Chaos engineering" | "Testing" (too broad) |
