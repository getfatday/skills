# Joshua Teter Vocabulary

| Term | Definition | Use This | Not This |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Connective tissue | The TPM's role binding teams, systems, and strategy together. The nervous system of an engineering organization. | "The TPM is the connective tissue" | "The TPM schedules meetings" |
| Influence without authority | Leading through data, relationships, and credibility rather than org-chart power. Active leadership, not passive suggestion. | "Lead through influence" | "Just suggest it and hope" |
| Technical depth | Ability to engage meaningfully in architectural discussions, read code, understand system trade-offs. The baseline for credibility. | "Do you have the technical depth?" | "Can you code the solution?" |
| Program vs. project | A program is a strategic collection of interdependent projects. A project is a bounded deliverable. Category error to conflate them. | "This is a program, not a project" | Using interchangeably |
| Mechanisms | Repeatable, scalable processes that produce consistent outcomes without depending on individuals. The opposite of hero culture. | "What mechanism prevents this?" | "Who will handle this?" |
| Blast radius | The scope of impact if a decision or risk materializes negatively. Forces you to think about *who* gets hit, not just *what*. | "What's the blast radius?" | "What's the risk?" |
| One-way door / two-way door | Irreversible vs. reversible decisions. One-way doors need senior sign-off and decision docs. Two-way doors can be made fast. (Amazon-influenced) | "Is this a one-way door?" | "How important is this decision?" |
| Communication architecture | The deliberate design of who communicates what, to whom, when, and how. Each channel has audience, purpose, format, cadence, owner. | "Design the communication architecture" | "Set up a Slack channel" |
| Risk register | A living document tracking risks with explicit owners, mitigation actions, due dates, and trigger conditions that convert risks to issues. | "Update the risk register" | "File it in the shared drive" |
| Strategic alignment | Ensuring program work maps directly to business outcomes and executive intent. Not vague association — explicit mapping required. | "Show me the strategic alignment" | "The VP asked for it" |
| Cross-functional orchestration | Coordinating work across teams with different managers, codebases, and priorities. The defining challenge of the TPM role. | "Orchestrate across teams" | "Manage the backlog" |
| Delivery accountability | Owning the outcome of whether the program ships, without owning the code or the people who write it. Accountability without direct authority. | "Who has delivery accountability?" | "Who's the tech lead?" |
| Ambiguity navigation | The skill of creating structure and clarity where none exists. Definitional to the TPM role — if everything were clear, you wouldn't need one. | "Navigate the ambiguity" | "Wait for requirements" |
| Relationship capital | Trust and credibility built over time through consistent delivery and genuine engagement. The currency that enables influence without authority. | "Invest in relationship capital" | "Network more" |
| Force multiplier | A skill or practice that amplifies the effectiveness of everything else. EI is a force multiplier for technical skills. | "EI is a force multiplier" | "EI is a nice-to-have" |
| Well-managed failures | Programs delivered on time and on budget that solve the wrong problem. The consequence of jumping to execution without strategic alignment. | "Avoid well-managed failures" | "We shipped on time" |
| Big Five TPM Comparison | Framework distinguishing TPM from 5 adjacent roles: PM, EM, ScrumMaster, Project Manager, non-technical Program Manager. | "Apply the Big Five comparison" | "TPM is like a PM but technical" |
| Communication cadence | The rhythm of communication across a program: daily standups, weekly syncs, monthly exec reviews, ad hoc escalation. Deliberately designed. | "What's the communication cadence?" | "When's the next meeting?" |
| Stakeholder Influence Map | Stakeholders mapped by power × interest, overlaid with disposition (advocate/neutral/skeptic/blocker). Revisit at phase transitions. | "Update the Influence Map" | "Who are the stakeholders?" |
