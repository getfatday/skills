# Program Management Principles — Antonio Nieto-Rodriguez

## 1. Projects Are the New Economic Engine
The world is shifting from an operations economy to a Project Economy. Value is created through temporary, unique initiatives — not repetitive operations. Organizations still structured for the operations era are structurally disadvantaged.

**When to apply:** Organizational design, resource allocation, strategic planning.
**Source:** The Project Revolution, HBR "The Project Economy"

## 2. Organizations Must Become Focused
Most organizations spread resources across too many projects. The Peanut Butter Problem: a thin layer on everything, thick on nothing. Cut the portfolio by 50-70%. The highest-performing organizations ruthlessly prioritize the vital few.

**When to apply:** Portfolio reviews, resource allocation, when someone proposes a new project.
**Source:** The Focused Organization

## 3. Strategy Without Projects Is Hallucination
Strategy execution IS project execution. The gap between strategy and results is a project management gap. Strategy teams create beautiful decks and hand off to execution — that gap is where strategies die.

**When to apply:** Strategic planning, when strategy and execution are organizationally separated.
**Source:** Brightline Initiative, HBR Handbook

## 4. Benefits Realization Is the Only Measure That Matters
Project success must be measured by whether promised benefits were actually realized — not by on-time/on-budget delivery. A system delivered to spec that nobody uses is a failure. Extend measurement 6-18 months post-delivery.

**When to apply:** Project governance, success criteria definition, steering committee reviews.
**Source:** HBR Handbook, The Focused Organization

## 5. Every Project Needs a Purpose, Not Just a Plan
Projects succeed when they start with "why." The Purpose Launchpad forces articulation of the problem, purpose, beneficiaries, and strategic alignment before any planning begins. Spend more time in problem definition — it's the highest-leverage activity.

**When to apply:** Project initiation, before any planning begins.
**Source:** HBR Handbook — Purpose Launchpad

## 6. Project Leadership ≠ Project Management
Management is control, tracking, and reporting. Leadership is inspiring, aligning stakeholders, and navigating ambiguity. The profession has over-indexed on management. The PM of the future is a change agent, not an administrator.

**When to apply:** Role definition, career development, when PM work becomes purely administrative.
**Source:** TEDx, PMI keynotes

## 7. Senior Leaders Must Own Projects
Executive sponsorship is the #1 predictor of project success. Sponsors must attend key meetings, remove blockers, champion publicly, and be accountable for benefits realization. Passive sign-off is not sponsorship.

**When to apply:** Project governance, sponsor selection, when projects lack executive engagement.
**Source:** HBR articles on sponsorship

## 8. Killing Projects Is as Important as Starting Them
Sunk cost fallacy and political dynamics keep zombie projects alive. Create a formal, blame-free process for project termination. Celebrate good kills — they free resources for projects that matter.

**When to apply:** Portfolio reviews, when a project shows persistent red status.
**Source:** HBR "How to Kill a Bad Project"

## 9. Power Skills Matter More Than Technical Skills
Communication, empathy, strategic thinking, and leadership outweigh Gantt charts and risk registers. Deliberately rebranded from "soft skills" to "power skills" to signal importance.

**When to apply:** Hiring, training, performance evaluation, when technical tools dominate the conversation.
**Source:** PMI chairmanship agenda, HBR Handbook

## 10. Prioritization Is the Most Undervalued Capability
The inability to say "no" to projects is the root cause of most organizational dysfunction. Limit active projects to organizational capacity, not ambition. Use the Prioritization Matrix: Quick Wins, Big Bets, Fill-Ins, Thankless Tasks (kill).

**When to apply:** Portfolio governance, when resource contention surfaces.
**Source:** The Focused Organization

## 11. Projects Should Be Treated as Investments, Not Costs
Shifting the mental model from "project cost" to "project investment with expected return" changes selection, funding, and governance. Finance vocabulary gives PM strategic credibility.

**When to apply:** Business cases, budget discussions, executive communication.
**Source:** The Focused Organization, HBR Handbook

## 12. The Project Manager of the Future Is a Change Agent
The role is evolving from administrative controller to strategic leader driving organizational transformation. Those who can't make this shift will be automated.

**When to apply:** Career development, organizational design, role evolution discussions.
**Source:** The Project Revolution, LinkedIn Newsletter
