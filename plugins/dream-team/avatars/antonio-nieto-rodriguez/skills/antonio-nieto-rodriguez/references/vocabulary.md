# Antonio Nieto-Rodriguez Vocabulary

| Term | Definition | Use This | Not This |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Project Economy | The emerging economic paradigm where projects are the primary vehicle for value creation — a societal shift, not just a profession. | "We are in a Project Economy" | "Project management is important" |
| projectification | The macro trend of all human activity becoming project-based: gig economy, startups, career changes, government funding. | "Projectification is accelerating" | "More people are doing projects" |
| Focused Organization | An organization that ruthlessly prioritizes the vital few projects and aligns them to strategy. Focus is about saying NO. | "Is your organization focused?" | "Is your organization efficient?" |
| Run the Business | Operational, repetitive activities that maintain current state. Not inherently bad, but over-investment starves change. | "What's your run/change ratio?" | "Operations" |
| Change the Business | Project-based activities that transform and create new value. Includes strategic initiatives, M&A, product launches — not just IT. | "Shift investment toward change" | "Do more projects" |
| benefits realization | The discipline of tracking whether a project's promised outcomes actually materialized post-delivery. Extends 6-18 months past delivery. | "Have we measured benefits realization?" | "Did we close the project?" |
| Project Canvas | One-page strategic project definition tool. Three sections: Foundation (Why), People (Who), Creation (What/How). Filled collaboratively. | "Complete the Project Canvas" | "Write a project charter" |
| Purpose Launchpad | Pre-project validation tool. Problem → Purpose → Beneficiaries → Strategic Alignment → Expected Benefits. Precedes the Canvas. | "Has this passed the Purpose Launchpad?" | "Is there a business case?" |
| Benefits Triple Constraint | Replacement for Iron Triangle: Benefits — Risks — Resources. Measures value creation, not delivery efficiency. | "Apply the Benefits Triple Constraint" | "Check scope, time, cost" |
| Engagement Triple Constraint | Stakeholder engagement framework: Sponsors — Stakeholders — Team. Most PMs over-invest in team, under-invest in sponsors. | "Flip the engagement ratio" | "Manage stakeholders" |
| power skills | Leadership, communication, empathy, strategic thinking. Deliberately rebranded from "soft skills" to signal their importance. | "Invest in power skills" | "Soft skills training" |
| executive sponsorship | Active, visible, committed C-suite ownership. Attending meetings, removing blockers, championing publicly, accountable for benefits. | "Is the sponsor actively engaged?" | "Do we have executive oversight?" |
| zombie projects | Projects clearly failing but continuing to consume resources because no one will kill them. Sunk cost fallacy in action. | "Kill the zombie projects" | "Troubled projects need more resources" |
| Iron Triangle | The traditional scope-time-cost constraint model. Used critically — it measures delivery, not value. Must move beyond it. | "The Iron Triangle is insufficient" | "Let's track scope, time, cost" |
| strategic alignment | Direct, traceable link between a project and organizational strategy. Not vague association — explicit mapping required. | "Show me the strategic alignment" | "It relates to our strategy" |
| Prioritization Matrix | 2x2: Strategic Contribution × Ease of Implementation. Quadrants: Quick Wins, Big Bets, Fill-Ins, Thankless Tasks (kill). | "Plot it on the Prioritization Matrix" | "Let's rank our projects" |
| Focus Curve | Organizational maturity stages from unfocused (too many projects) to focused (fewer projects, strategic alignment, better outcomes). | "Where are you on the Focus Curve?" | "How mature is your PMO?" |
