# Program Management Anti-Patterns — Antonio Nieto-Rodriguez

## The Peanut Butter Problem
**Signal:** "We have 50 active projects and resources for 10."
**Why it fails:** Resources spread like peanut butter — thin layer on everything, thick on nothing. Every project moves slowly, none deliver real impact.
**Correction:** Cut the portfolio by 50-70%. Do fewer projects, do them well. Prioritization Matrix: Quick Wins, Big Bets, Fill-Ins, Thankless Tasks (kill).
**Source:** The Focused Organization

## Iron Triangle Worship
**Signal:** "The project was on time, on scope, and on budget — it was successful."
**Why it fails:** A new IT system delivered perfectly to spec that nobody uses. Delivery metrics without benefits measurement is organizational theater.
**Correction:** Replace the Iron Triangle with the Benefits Triple Constraint: Benefits — Risks — Resources. Measure realized outcomes.
**Source:** HBR Handbook

## Absent Executive Sponsors
**Signal:** "The sponsor signed the charter and we haven't seen them since."
**Why it fails:** Without active sponsorship, projects lack political cover, blocker removal, and organizational alignment. The #1 predictor of failure.
**Correction:** Sponsors must attend key meetings, champion publicly, remove blockers, and be accountable for benefits realization.
**Source:** HBR articles on sponsorship

## PM as Administrative Overhead
**Signal:** "The PM just sends status reports and updates the Gantt chart."
**Why it fails:** Reduces the role to clerk. Strips away strategic value. Organizations get administrative compliance instead of leadership.
**Correction:** Elevate PM to strategic leadership. The PM should be a peer of functional leaders, not a subordinate.
**Source:** TEDx, PMI

## Solutions Before Problems
**Signal:** "We need a new CRM" without defining the customer problem.
**Why it fails:** Jumping to solutions produces well-specified projects that solve the wrong problem.
**Correction:** Use the Purpose Launchpad. Problem → Purpose → Beneficiaries → Strategic Alignment. Spend more time in problem definition.
**Source:** HBR Handbook

## Busyness as Productivity
**Signal:** Packed calendars, heroic 80-hour weeks, too many meetings celebrated as progress.
**Why it fails:** "Being busy is the opposite of being focused. Busy organizations are unfocused organizations."
**Correction:** Focus. Fewer projects, fewer meetings, clear priorities. Measure outcomes, not activity.
**Source:** The Focused Organization

## Ignoring Project Failure Rates
**Signal:** "60-70% failure rate is just how it is."
**Why it fails:** Acceptance normalizes trillions of dollars in waste. Treats systemic organizational dysfunction as individual PM incompetence.
**Correction:** Treat failure as a systemic problem. Fix organizational structures, prioritization, and sponsorship — not just PM skills.
**Source:** TEDx, The Project Revolution

## Methodology Obsession
**Signal:** "Should we use Agile or Waterfall?" consuming months of debate.
**Why it fails:** "The methodology wars are a distraction. Projects are delivered by people, not frameworks."
**Correction:** Focus on power skills: leadership, communication, stakeholder engagement. Methodology is secondary.
**Source:** PMI chairmanship, HBR Handbook

## Failing to Kill Projects
**Signal:** Red status for 6 months, no one willing to terminate.
**Why it fails:** Sunk cost fallacy. Political dynamics where no one admits failure. Zombie projects consume resources that could fund projects that matter.
**Correction:** Formal, blame-free termination process. Celebrate good kills. Quarterly portfolio reviews with explicit kill decisions.
**Source:** HBR "How to Kill a Bad Project"

## Separating Strategy from Execution
**Signal:** Strategy team creates 50-slide deck, hands off to "implementation team."
**Why it fails:** Strategy dies in the gap. Strategists don't stay involved. Executors don't understand intent.
**Correction:** Strategy IS execution. Use the Project Canvas to make strategy tangible. Strategists stay through delivery.
**Source:** Brightline Initiative
